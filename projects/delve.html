Delve & the Office Graph

Win8 app, web

Role

Senior UX Design Manager

Year

2012–2016

Tools

Adobe Creative Suite

Platform

Web, Windows 8

Overview

Coming from the Enterprise Search team in SharePoint and former Fast Search, the Microsoft Development Center in Oslo pioneered a new way of discovering people and information at work.

The Office Graph (later Microsoft Graph) introduced a new model for discovery by mapping relationships between people, documents, and activities across Microsoft 365. Instead of relying only on queries and metadata, experiences could surface relevant information proactively based on signals from collaboration patterns and the social graph at work.

I led a small design team and worked closely with PMs and engineers to build Delve, the first user experience powered by the Office Graph. The concept was introduced as a major innovation at the SharePoint Conference in 2014, and later at Microsoft Ignite 2015. Delve demonstrated how insights from the Office Graph could proactively surface documents, conversations, and people that were relevant to your work.

My work spanned both product and communication; UX design for Delve Windows 8 app, and later web experience, and the visual storytelling around the concept, including visuals and presentation material. These visuals were later adopted by Microsoft's marketing teams and shown on XL stage displays during the conference keynote.

At the time, Delve represented a significant shift in enterprise productivity tools. It moved beyond traditional search toward contextual and proactive discovery, leveraging signals from collaboration and organizational networks. The work also contributed to a series of patents around the Delve user experience, particularly in how information can be surfaced and navigated through graph-based relationships.

The earliest prototype of Delve (then called Project Oslo) was demonstrated as a Windows 8 app using the Metro design language. During this period, I also built my first Windows Phone application as part of the broader ecosystem exploration. Delve later evolved into a web-based experience integrated into Office 365, becoming a central hub for discovering documents and colleagues across the suite.

While Delve as a standalone product was retired in 2024, graph-powered discovery, contextual recommendations, and people-centric navigation of information continue to live on across Microsoft 365 experiences.
